Compliance reminder

Mesenchymal Stem Cell Allograft, Exosomes, and Secretomes are Human Cell and Tissue Products (HCT/Ps) regulated under Section 361 of the U.S. Public Health Service Act — they are not FDA-approved drugs. Never describe them as "FDA-approved," a "cure," or as having "guaranteed results." Position them as advanced regenerative biologics, backed by strict U.S. sourcing, screening, and processing standards. Full guidance in the appendix.

Objections & Compliance

Quick Objection-Handling Reference

"We already have a supplier."

Ask what they value most (sourcing, price, exosome concentration, support) and offer a side-by-side comparison or trial order.

"Is this FDA-approved?"

Clarify: these are HCT/Ps regulated under Section 361 of the U.S. Public Health Service Act, not FDA-approved drugs. Emphasize CLIA-certified processing and donor screening standards. Avoid guaranteeing outcomes.

"It's too expensive."

Reframe around per-treatment value/margin for the clinic, or offer a trial-size order to lower the initial commitment.

"I need to check with my partner/associate."

Offer to present to the full group directly, or send materials both can review together.

"Not the right time."

Send the intro video/one-pager now and set a specific follow-up date rather than leaving it open-ended.

Do's and Don'ts for Compliance

Do

  • Describe products as "regenerative biologic support" or "cellular and tissue support products," backed by science and strict sourcing standards.
  • Mention CLIA-certified processing and donor screening as trust signals.

Don't

  • Promise cures, guaranteed outcomes, or specific clinical results.
  • Refer to the products as "FDA-approved" — they are HCT/Ps under Section 361 PHSA, not approved drugs.
